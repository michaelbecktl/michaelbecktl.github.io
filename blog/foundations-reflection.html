Foundation's Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

One of the few things I've learned about myself through our journey through human skills, is how my personal values have stuck with me throughout a majority of my adult life. These values have been the foundation to how I behave, learn, and grow as an individual. With the base of those foundations, I've also learned about how emotional intelligence relates around my life and how it affects my growth and interactions with my former colleagues. Throughout my foundations learning journey, I've definitely felt imposter's syndrome very often when faced with difficulties during some of the tech sections. I've only become more determined to make sure I don't feel that way again.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I feel that having values gives you a foundation to have a condusive mindset in learning new things and as an overall improve our own performance and capabilities in anything, including programming. Empathy and self-awareness is key in developing patience in not just another but with ourselves when reflecting on work and progress.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

While I've understood mostly that human skills is related to our own behaviours and how we interact with others in daily lives and in workspaces, I would not have imagined diving deep into aspects like mindfulness and neuroplasticity. While it hasn't really struck me entirely, I can see how it plays it's role in the future.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

I think the most challenging thing is not understanding Human Skills, but actually learning to implement them proactively into our work. As we age we tend to not stray away from routine and our behavioral norms, but as with neuroplasticity, we can change if we work on it and believe there's always room for improvement.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Technical skills are important to any job industry, but I've always felt that human skills were something that was ignored in past decades. In the present age, human skills are becoming more of an important factor in hiring employees. These human skills are also benefictial in learning the actual tech itself as it teaches us to pace ourselves and set up a healthy learning plan.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don't feel like the time spent studying Human Skills was a waste of time. Admittedly, I have screened past the human skills sections in a short time though not because I didn't care about them, but the sections were fairly straightforward and as long as I understand the concept of what was taught, I felt like I good move forward. With that said, the time spent on human skills wasn't too much, and I felt a good balance of it compared to the time spent on learning tech sections.
