Identity, Values, and Strengths

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I'm not sure what kind of situation I would use as an example, as I don't feel like I've made a big ethical decision. When I was still studying Cookery in New Zealand, there was an assignment that we worked on that was relatively difficult for some of my classmates. A friend had then approached me and asked for guidance with his work, but I was unfortunately occupied with family matters at the time. He asked me if he could look at my work and perhaps get some idea on how to work around it. Trusting him, I sent him a copy of my work. Funny enough, I even said "Don't copy my stuff yeah", which he just say "yeah, of course".

Not surprisingly, the next day our tutor called on me for plagiarism and I had to re-do my work while he did not have to. Looking back at the situation, it was obviously unfair that I was the one that had to start over. Thinking deeply, the tutor would've known my work was the original, and this was a lesson on having some integrity and not handing over your work to someone else. The more I think about it, I've made the wrong decision and perhaps "not" helping someone sometimes, could actually help them to grow into learning how to self-develop than relying on others.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I was born in Malaysia, and my cultural background is Chinese. Having three older brothers that have shaped my life revolving around old-fashioned values, which I still hold true to today. Family has been the center of my life for most of what I could remember, no matter how far off we stray or how occupied we are with our personal schedule, there was always time to spend with family and we appreciate the small moments we have together in recent days.

Being was raised in a multiracial environment, I was surrounded by many different people and culture, we've learned to be respectful towards one another regardless of how different people are. As I myself had learnt to speak in English, Malay and Cantonese, the nuance in languages and also exposure to different people. With learning multiple languages, not everyone including myself could be a completely fluent bilingual or trilingual, this had molded me to try and understand people between the lines rather than the face value of their speech. I'd actually find myself misunderstanding friends and strangers a lot when I was younger, despite knowing the language, I found myself in a language barrier! "Why?" I thought, it was only as I graduated from high-school that I have learned to keep an open-mind, as expression is not necessary well-formed in speech and sentences, but it is from the heart.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I am a very consistent and persistent person when it comes to learning and progression, which enables me to face challenges comfortably and if anything; enjoy them. Throughout my 11 years experience as a Chef, I've grown used to high-pressure environments and have gained the ability to lead and problem-solve in a calm and organized demeanor. This would help me in planning my learning journey in complicated obstacles and finding alternatives methods to resolving issues.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

While I think I am a very patient person in general circumstances, when it comes to progression and self-development, I tend to be very impatient. While this does not necessarily translate to prefer quicker methods in learning things, I may tend to miss out details when reading through materials.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

There was a time when the team leader of another department was constantly berating her team members, decreasing overall team morale over miniscule detail. The department was very closely related to mine and eventually the tension had seeped into my team especially during times when I was absent. The overall productivity as a result of this tension had been poor between both our teams, so I looked into resolving the matter. First, I had to identify what is the cause of the other leader's behaviour and tried to assess what had prompted her verbally aggressive behaviour onto her team. I gathered testimony from both the leader and employees that had worked with her, then closely monitored the workflow on subsequent days. In the end, I found that the leader's behaviour was without reasonable means and has been emotionally unstable.

I've tried to confront her and teach her alternative methods to lead or train her team, then was responded with claims that her team members were incompetent, but she would try her best to calm down. As time gradually passed, I saw no improvement in the team synergy and her leadership, I had no choice but to submit a report to the Human Resource department. Looking back at how I handled the situation, I believe it was the most reasonable thing to do at the time. However, I thought I should've escalated the matter to management a lot sooner rather than taking the slow approach to properly understood the issue. As productivity was low and I did not realized how impactful it was.
