My Learning Plan

My goal is to bridge my career path into the information technology sector. I am determined to come out at the end of this course, equipped with necessary knowledge and continue in my own self-development in terms of human skills and technical skills. I've found myself interested and invested in topics I enjoy particularly difficult and challenging ones, and these challenges drive me to learn and overcome them.

My strength is that I'm fueled by failure and mistakes. Imperfection and inconsistency sets me in a path to further improve my skills. In terms of limitations, I have a poor attention span if the topic is too lengthy and basic, impatience can often times just lead to procrastination or being distracted from my own learning plan. I'm also a married man, that wants to be able to spend as much time with my spouse as possible, as well as putting time into my own personal hobbies. My personal hours being in balance keeps me motivated to move forward in life.

Going forward, I hope to learn how to work together with others and improve my communication skills and my organization skills. Prior to this course, I've often been assigned lead roles without co-leaders or given much supervision myself. While I can handle myself, I want to receive more exposure when it comes to leading a task side-by-side with others, rather than being the one that's always steering the line.

I've set a fixed time to work and study. While I understand the course dynamic allows us a flexible schedule, I find it weirdly accommodating when I put myself into work/studies at a traditional time schedule. I intend to also communicate with others more, and ask more questions or advice from fellow peers and facilitators. If in any case, work starts to pile up and become difficult, I'm able to prioritize and focus on my task while setting a personal deadline. Then if I found myself struggling at a certain checkpoint for too long, I'll seek some advice from the facilitators or other students on Discord.

I hope the facilitation team is able to guide me on areas I can personally improve in both technical and human skills. while I don't want to receive flat-out answers to problems I face, a review or feedback of my work would greatly help.

In terms of time schedule, I'm still currently working at my previous workplace as a part-timer. I have officially resigned from my position with my last day ending just before bootcamp, so that I can dedicate my time into it.
