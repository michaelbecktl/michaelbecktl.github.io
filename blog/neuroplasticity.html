Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

Neuroplasticity is about how the human brain is able to grow and adapt to situations on a daily basis, regardless of a person's age. It isn't uncommon for people, particularly adults, that believe they are always born with a limit or talent. People tend to believe that they are meant for something, while at the same time they are unable to learn about things that aren't in their forte. The understanding of neuroplasticity shows people that they can learn new things. Understanding that through practice, their brain would be able to adapt and learn; people would be more encouraged and determined to overcome challenges and keep learning.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

I want to attempt more challenges, and keep trying new things. Overcoming things and learning how to do it is my method of practice and developing neuroplasticity. Practice and repetition is also a great way to strengthen memories and understanding of concepts. Essentially, sinking time into learning and practice is the best way for me.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Health is important in balance to frequent practice and studying. Taking a break once in a while, having proper sleep hours and engaging in physical exercise can also help stimulate the brain to adapt.

Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

The growth mindset is the thought process and behavioral pattern of an individual that reflects their ability and attributes towards learning. A person with growth mindset is more likely to take on difficult tasks with persistence because of the eagerness to learn. It is directly correlated with neuroplasticity, as a person with growth mindset is consistently stimulating neuroplasticity.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I've also learned about fixed mindset, and tried to reflect on it's common behaviour pattern against my own habits. I think there are definitely room for improvements in myself and I'd like to put myself in a better growth mindset.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I will set a goal for myself, and challenge myself to get through the day at a timely schedule. With habitual learning I believe I can learn at a more effective pace.
